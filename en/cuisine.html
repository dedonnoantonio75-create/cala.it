Our Philosophy

Food as an Expression of Place

At Cala dei Balcani, food is not simply catering — it is one of the principal ways in which we share the spirit of Salento with our guests. Our philosophy is straightforward and uncompromising: every ingredient must be sourced locally, seasonally, and with the same care for provenance that we bring to every other aspect of a celebration here.

Salento is one of Italy's great, underappreciated food regions. The combination of a sun-drenched Mediterranean climate, fertile red earth — the terra rossa — and a coastline of extraordinary biodiversity produces ingredients of exceptional quality: olive oil with a peppery intensity that makes the finest Tuscan oils seem mild by comparison, cheeses aged in natural caves, seafood pulled from waters so clear you can see the seabed from the surface. We consider it a privilege to work with these ingredients and a responsibility to present them with skill and respect.

From the Sea

The Adriatic Larder

The Adriatic waters off Santa Cesarea Terme are among the cleanest and most biodiverse in the Mediterranean. Local fishermen — many of them working the same waters their grandfathers worked — bring in catches that form the foundation of our seafood offering. What arrives in our kitchen each morning is determined by what the sea has offered, not by what a supplier's catalogue suggests.

Polpo alla pignata — slow-braised octopus cooked in terracotta — is one of the great dishes of Salento. Tender, intensely savoury, perfumed with wild fennel and local wine, it is a preparation that rewards patience and good ingredients equally. Our version has become one of the dishes most requested by couples who have attended previous weddings here.

Ricci di mare — sea urchins — served simply, cracked open and offered on crushed ice with a wedge of lemon and a slice of toasted bread, are one of the purest pleasures the Adriatic offers. The experience of tasting a fresh riccio di mare within sight of the sea it came from is one that guests from all over the world consistently describe as a revelation.

Gamberi rossi del Mediterraneo — Mediterranean red prawns, caught at depth in the waters of the Ionian and Adriatic — are presented both raw, as a crudo, and lightly grilled, to allow the extraordinary sweetness of the flesh to speak for itself. They are among the finest crustaceans in the world, and they thrive in these waters.

From the Land

The Masseria Table

Caciocavallo podolico is the king of southern Italian cheeses. Produced from the milk of the Podolica — a hardy, semi-wild breed of cattle that grazes the upland pastures of the Murgia and the Salentine interior — it develops over months of aging a complexity that rivals the finest aged cheeses of Europe. Firm, aromatic, with notes of wild herbs, dried fruit, and the particular mineral quality of the limestone pastures, it is a cheese that rewards serious attention. We source ours from small producers with whom we have built relationships over many years.

Friselle are the bread of Salento — hard, twice-baked rings of durum wheat that have been the staple of farmers and fishermen for centuries. Briefly soaked in seawater or well water to soften them slightly, then topped with fresh tomatoes crushed by hand, olive oil, salt, and wild oregano, they represent the essence of Salentine simplicity: a dish that requires almost nothing, yet — when made with the right ingredients — achieves a perfection that elaborate cooking rarely matches. We serve friselle throughout our events as a connection to the deepest roots of this food culture.

Our vegetable courses draw on the extraordinary produce of the local masserie — the great farm estates of the Salentine interior — where tomatoes, aubergines, peppers, and bitter greens are grown in soils enriched by centuries of careful cultivation. Each season brings its own character to our tables, and we celebrate rather than resist these changes.

The Cellar

Salentine Wines

Salento is one of Italy's most exciting wine regions — a fact that the international wine world has been discovering with increasing enthusiasm over the past two decades. The combination of intense sun, low rainfall, old-vine Negroamaro and Primitivo grapes, and the skill of a new generation of winemakers has produced wines of extraordinary depth, structure, and originality. We have curated a selection that showcases both the classic expressions of these indigenous varieties and the most innovative contemporary interpretations.

Negroamaro

The great red grape of Salento. Deep, dark, structured wines with notes of ripe cherry, tobacco, Mediterranean scrub and a distinctive bitter finish that Italians call "amaro nobile". Best from the Copertino and Guagnano appellations.

Primitivo di Manduria

Rich, opulent, high-alcohol reds from one of Puglia's most celebrated DOC appellations. The Primitivo grape — genetically identical to Zinfandel — produces wines of extraordinary warmth and generosity, perfect for the long tables of a summer wedding reception.

Verdeca & Bianco di Leverano

The white wines of Salento are perhaps its best-kept secret. Crisp, mineral, with the refreshing acidity of grapes grown in calcareous soil, they are perfect companions to the seafood courses that open our menus. Served ice-cold as the sun still sits high over the Adriatic.
